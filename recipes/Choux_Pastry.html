Choux Pastry

Easy Choux Pastry Recipe: Perfect for Eclairs, Profiteroles, and More!

Making choux pastry, also known as pâte à choux, is remarkably simple using butter, hot water, flour, and eggs. The dough can be prepared in under 15 minutes and when baked, it steams and expands, resulting in delightful treats like eclairs, profiteroles, choux pastry rings, savory gougères, and numerous other delicious options!

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup water
  • 1/4 cup butter, cut into small cubes
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten

Steps

  1. Combine water, butter, sugar, and salt in a medium saucepan over medium heat; cook until butter has melted and mixture comes to a boil.
  2. Add flour all at once; stir vigorously with a wooden spoon over medium-low heat until mixture forms a ball and the saucepan is coated in a light film of flour, about 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Remove the pan from heat and let cool for 10 minutes.
  4. Beat in eggs, one at a time, with the wooden spoon until the mixture forms a sticky dough with a pipeable consistency.
